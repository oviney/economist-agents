# Editorial Illustration Skill

## Purpose
Define the visual standard for featured images and charts generated
by the content pipeline.  Every image must look like it was commissioned
by The Economist's art department, not generated by a free icon tool.

## Reference
Study The Economist's actual illustrations at economist.com.  Key
characteristics:

- **Bold, high-contrast graphic editorial illustration** — strong
  geometric compositions, confident lines, NOT painterly or textured
- **Human figures or symbolic objects** — stylised people in suits, at
  desks, in boardrooms, or conceptual objects representing ideas
- **Bold, limited colour palette** — Economist red (#E3120B), strong
  blues, navy, high contrast, colour blocking
- **Conceptual but specific** — the illustration tells a story about
  the article's argument through visual metaphor
- **Editorial wit** — subtle visual irony, unexpected juxtapositions,
  a slightly satirical tone
- **Minimal or abstract backgrounds** — NOT busy environments.
  Clean, confident compositions.

## What We're Getting Wrong

Current DALL-E prompt produces:
- Flat minimalist vector shapes (❌ looks like a tech blog)
- 3-colour limited palette of navy/burgundy/beige (❌ too restrictive)
- Generic metaphorical objects — gears, arrows, shields (❌ cliché)
- No human presence (❌ cold and impersonal)
- Icon-like simplicity (❌ not editorial quality)

## What We Need

### Style Direction
```
Bold, high-contrast graphic editorial illustration.
Think The Economist's iconic cover art — strong geometric compositions,
confident lines, conceptual metaphors, colour blocking.
NOT watercolour.  NOT oil painting.  NOT photorealism.  NOT pastel.
NOT painterly brushstrokes.  NOT textured canvas.
```

### Composition Rules
- **Always include a human element** — a person, hands, a face,
  a silhouette.  Humans make illustrations relatable.
- **Scene, not symbol** — show a situation, not an icon.  A person
  at a desk overwhelmed by screens, not a generic "technology" symbol.
- **Scale and perspective** — use exaggerated scale to make a point.
  A tiny person next to an enormous machine.  A massive hand pushing
  a small building.
- **Minimal or abstract backgrounds** — flat colour blocks, clean
  negative space, NOT detailed environments.

### Colour Palette (Bold and Limited)
Use bold colour blocking with a limited palette:
- **Primary accent**: Economist red (#E3120B)
- **Strong blues**: #3b6d8f, #17648d, navy
- **High-contrast darks**: Charcoal (#333333), deep navy (#1a1a2e)
- **Accent**: Dusty red (#a34054), ochre (#c4953a)
- **Background**: Clean white, off-white (#f4f0e6), or flat colour blocks
- **Shadows**: Deep charcoal, not black

### Size and Format
- **Aspect ratio**: 1792x1024 (landscape, blog hero format)
- **Quality**: HD
- **No text**: Absolutely no words, letters, numbers, or labels

## DALL-E 3 Prompt Template

```
Create an editorial illustration in the style of The Economist magazine's
bold, graphic cover art and editorial imagery.

STYLE: Bold, high-contrast graphic editorial illustration.
Strong geometric compositions with confident lines and shapes.
Conceptual metaphor: human figures or symbolic objects representing ideas.
NOT watercolour, NOT oil painting, NOT photorealism, NOT soft brushstrokes.

SCENE: [Describe a specific scene that embodies the article's argument.
Include at least one human figure. Use a minimal or abstract background.]

COLOUR: Bold, limited palette. Economist red (#E3120B) accent.
Strong blues, navy, high-contrast darks. Colour blocking.
Clean white or off-white background.

MOOD: [Satirical / contemplative / urgent / ironic — match the article's
tone]

COMPOSITION: Strong geometric composition with clear visual hierarchy.
Clear dominant foreground subject. Minimal background.
Use scale exaggeration to make the editorial point.
Generous negative space on one side for text overlay.

CRITICAL: No text, words, letters, numbers, or symbols anywhere in
the image. No labels, no signs, no captions.
```

## Chart and Graph Standards

### When to Include Charts
- Data-driven articles with quantitative trends → YES
- Conceptual/strategic articles → NO (featured image only)
- If including a chart, it must be referenced naturally in the text

### Chart Style (Economist Standard)
- **Colour**: Navy (#17648d) primary, burgundy (#843844) accent
- **Typography**: Clean sans-serif labels
- **Grid**: Minimal — horizontal lines only, no vertical grid
- **Legend**: Inline labels, not a separate legend box
- **Source**: Small source attribution at bottom
- **No 3D effects**: Ever
- **White background**: Clean, uncluttered

### Chart Types by Data
- Time series → line chart
- Comparison → horizontal bar chart
- Composition → stacked bar or treemap
- Correlation → scatter plot
- Never: pie charts, radar charts, 3D bar charts
